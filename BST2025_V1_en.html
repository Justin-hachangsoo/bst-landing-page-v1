6.1 External Audit Schedule

6.2 Formal Verification

BST applies Formal Verification to ensure that core on-chain logic—staking, reward distribution, and governance—always behaves as specified. This is designed to eliminate major risks in advance, such as exceeding the max supply, reward miscalculations, and privilege abuse.

  • Key Invariants (Examples): total_supply ≤ MAX_SUPPLY, pool_balance ≥ Σ claimable_reward[u], staked_amount[u] does not decrease before unstaking, all parameter changes follow the approved governance process (proposal → vote → timelock → execute), tokenized_rwa ≤ oracle_value × collateral_ratio
  • Verification Process: (1) Specify invariants/pre/post conditions → (2) Rust (Anchor) annotations & TLA+ modeling → (3) Automatic proofs with SMT solvers / model checkers (Kani, Prusti, etc.) → (4) Fuzzing (cargo-fuzz) & unit tests → (5) CI pipeline (GitHub Actions) runs verification on every PR

Disclaimer — Formal verification guarantees completeness only within the defined specification scope. Oracle/off-chain data and user key management are handled by separate security processes. Verification results are transparently disclosed in public reports whenever contracts change.

6.3 Insurance Integration

BST mitigates core on-chain risks—smart contract vulnerabilities, bridge/oracle failures, etc.—by integrating coverage with decentralized insurance protocols such as Nexus Mutual and InsurAce. Coverage scope, period, and premiums (e.g., ~1–4% per year) are set by DAO decisions, and coverage status is automatically renewed after contract upgrades or major parameter changes.

  • Coverage Scope: Smart contract exploits/bugs, bridge & oracle failures, stablecoin de-pegs
  • Integrated Protocols: Nexus Mutual (Smart Contract Cover), InsurAce (Smart Contract Vulnerability / De-peg Cover)
  • Premiums & Limits: Typically ~1–4% annually (varies by limit, duration, and risk score)
  • Acquisition/Maintenance: DAO treasury buys base coverage; users may purchase optional additional coverage
  • Claims Process: Incident → Submit on-chain evidence → Insurance protocol community/committee approval → Compensation distribution
  • Transparency: Coverage targets, premium spending, and claim results are reported regularly and reflected in governance

Exclusions & Limits — Coverage applies only within defined scopes; off-chain risks such as private key loss/phishing are excluded. Coverage scope may change per each protocol’s policy and will be announced through DAO governance upon updates.

7.2 Auto-Optimization Vaults

BST provides strategy vaults that connect multiple Solana-based DeFi programs to maximize automatic rebalancing and compound (reinvestment) effects. Assets inside the vault combine Liquid Staking tokens and Hybrid Staking products (maintaining liquidity + fixed lock-up) to secure both liquidity and yield. In particular, Hybrid Staking adopts a multi-chain staking model and has no vesting, enabling immediate liquidity, which is expected to drive explosive initial and sustained demand.

  • Strategy Engine: Automatically adjusts asset weights in real time by analyzing on-chain metrics such as APR, TVL, slippage, fees
  • Compounding Structure: Periodically auto-reinvests accrued rewards to accumulate returns
  • Risk Management: Switches to a conservative mode when oracle anomalies/protocol irregularities occur; adheres to predefined risk limits
  • Governance Alignment: Strategy changes/parameter updates follow the DAO proposal → vote → timelock procedure
  • Transparency: Vault performance, fees, and reinvestment logs are openly published on-chain via dashboard
  • Hybrid Staking Highlights: Multi-chain participation, no vesting → instant liquidity + strong participation incentives
